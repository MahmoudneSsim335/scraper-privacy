Privacy Policy — Scraper (Chrome Extension)

Effective date: August 26, 2025

Scraper is a Chrome extension by CresenGrowth that lets you highlight elements on a web page and export the selected data into structured formats (e.g., CSV or spreadsheets). This policy describes what data the extension accesses, how it’s used, and the choices available to you.

2) What we do not collect

  • No collection of passwords, payment information, or personal communications.
  • No collection or sale of personally identifiable information (PII) by default.
  • No background tracking of browsing history, mouse movements, keystrokes, or unrelated page data.
  • By default, Scraper does not transmit scraped page content to CresenGrowth servers.

3) Permissions used

  • tabs: identify the active tab to operate on the page you’re viewing.
  • scripting: inject content scripts that read the DOM elements you highlight.
  • contextMenus: show right-click actions (e.g., “Scrape this element”).
  • clipboardWrite: copy extracted data to your clipboard when requested.
  • storage: store local preferences and states.
  • Host permissions: access the DOM only on pages where you choose to run Scraper.
  • Optional identity: Google sign‑in for settings sync.

4) Remote code & third parties

To keep the extension lightweight and promptly fix issues, Scraper may load libraries from CresenGrowth‑controlled endpoints over HTTPS. We do not include third‑party advertising or analytics SDKs. If you connect Scraper to an integration you choose (e.g., export to an external service), data is sent only to that service under your action and is governed by that service’s policy.

5) How we use data

  • Provide, maintain, and improve core scraping and export functionality.
  • Remember preferences and optional sync settings.
  • Comply with legal obligations and security requirements.

6) Sharing and sale

We do not sell user data. We do not share data with third parties except (a) services you opt into (e.g., Google for sync), (b) service providers acting on our behalf under confidentiality, or (c) when required by law.

7) Security

We follow reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect hosted assets and minimize data flows. Any data you export to third‑party tools or cloud storage is subject to those services’ security and privacy practices.

8) Your choices & controls

  • Permissions: You can adjust site access and extension permissions in Chrome.
  • Clear data: Remove local data via chrome://settings/siteData → “View permissions and data stored across sites” or by uninstalling the extension.
  • Sync: If enabled, turn off Chrome sync or sign out to stop syncing extension settings.
  • Uninstall: Remove the extension at any time from chrome://extensions.

9) Legal bases (EU/UK GDPR)

  • Performance of a contract: to provide the functionality you request.
  • Legitimate interests: to improve reliability and security, and to store preferences.
  • Consent: for optional features like sign‑in/sync or integrations you explicitly enable.

10) Children’s privacy

Scraper is not intended for children under 13 and does not knowingly collect children’s personal information.

11) International transfers

If optional services you enable involve processing outside your region, we rely on appropriate safeguards such as standard contractual clauses where applicable.

We may update this policy from time to time. We will update the “Effective date” and publish the latest version at this URL.
